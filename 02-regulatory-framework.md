# Regulatory Framework

Lend focuses on the tokenization of **real estate-backed bond instruments structured under French regulatory frameworks**.

Each OpLend token represents a fractional exposure to a real-world financing structure managed by the Lend platform.

## Tokenized Real Estate Bonds

In the Lend model, each token corresponds to a fraction of a real estate-backed obligation issued through a compliant financing structure.

The smallest decimal fraction of a token is directly linked to the underlying financial instrument, allowing precise representation of investor exposure.

## Asset Sourcing and Management

All operations are sourced, analyzed and managed internally by Lend’s **real estate asset management team**.

Each investment opportunity goes through a full underwriting process including:

- financial analysis
- asset valuation
- risk assessment
- legal structuring

## Identity and Compliance Controls

In order to comply with regulatory requirements associated with real estate-backed financial instruments, Lend implements identity and compliance checks for all participating investors.

Each wallet address interacting with the protocol must be associated with a declared identity including a legal name and residential address. This information is required because each tokenized bond position must ultimately be linked to a real-world investor identity.

Before allowing participation in an operation, Lend performs compliance screening on the provided information, including sanctions and AML checks against relevant databases. This ensures that the platform maintains appropriate compliance standards while enabling efficient onboarding of investors.

The identity verification process is designed to be lightweight and compatible with blockchain-native user flows. Rather than relying on a fully custodial onboarding process, the system relies on declarative identity information linked to the investor wallet and validated through compliance screening. This approach allows the protocol to maintain the necessary regulatory linkage between tokenized securities and investor identities while preserving a streamlined user experience.

## Regulatory Oversight

For every financing operation, a complete investment dossier (DIS) is prepared and submitted to the **Autorité des Marchés Financiers (AMF)**, the French financial regulator.

This process ensures that each operation benefits from appropriate regulatory oversight and provides a strong level of investor protection.

By combining blockchain-based tokenization with traditional financial supervision, Lend aims to create compliant tokenized investment products accessible to crypto-native investors.
